## 1. What Is Quantum Incursion?

Quantum Incursion (QI) is a cross-platform cooperative guild feature introduced on February 1, 2024 (beta) and released live on March 21, 2024. Originally called "Guild Raids" during development, it requires players to:
- Unlock the **Chain of Command** technology
- Be a member of a guild

Each guild works together to advance through a branching map of encounters — combat nodes, resource donation nodes, and boss fights — across progressively harder difficulty levels, earning personal rewards and contributing to guild-wide progress used in inter-guild competition.

## 2. Cycles / Season Structure

### Season
- Duration: **11 active days**, beginning on a Thursday
- Break: **3-day break** after each season
- Total cycle: **14 days per season**

### Championship
- **6 consecutive seasons** form one Championship (~84 days / ~3 months total)
- Guilds compete on a leaderboard across the full championship
- The Quantum Pass tracks individual progress across the entire championship (does not reset between seasons)

### Difficulty Progression
- Guilds can advance through **up to 12 difficulty levels** per season
- From the 4th Championship onward: the active difficulty **automatically advances daily at midnight** server time, cycling through difficulties 1–12 over the course of the season

### Map Path History
- Original (Iron Age): **Basilisk Peaks**
- 4th Championship added: **Obsidian Peaks**, **Tempest Spires**, **Onyx Crater**
- Championship 1, 2025: unified into a single path — **"The Gauntlet"**
- Middle Ages (Oct 2025+): New themed maps with Middle Ages visual style

## 3. The Quantum Settlement

A completely separate mini-city, rebuilt from scratch each season. Produces resources and units used exclusively within QI. No main-city resources carry over.

### Starting Resources (per season)
- 150,000 Quantum Coins
- 25,000 Quantum Supplies
- 500 Quantum Shards (at start of a new Championship)
- 20 of each Quantum Goods type
- 8 units each of Artillery, Fast, Light, Heavy, and Ranged

### Building Selection at Season Start
Players choose which building types to use. Selection is locked for the entire season and resets at the next season.

| Era | Goods Selection | Military Selection |
|---|---|---|
| Iron Age | 2 of 5 types | 2 of 5 types |
| Middle Ages | 3 of 5 types | 3 of 5 unit categories (grants all buildings of that type across all three sub-eras) |

### Always-Available Building Categories (no selection required)
- Residential buildings
- Production buildings
- Cultural buildings
- Decorations

## 4. Settlement Buildings

### Town Hall
- Fixed, always present
- **Iron Age:** Produces Quantum Coins + Supplies every 10 hours
- **Middle Ages:** Produces 50,000 Quantum Coins + 50,000 Quantum Supplies + 15 Chrono Alloys per 10 hours
- Town Hall production is **NOT affected by Euphoria**

### Residential Buildings
Provide population and a secondary bonus:
- **Iron Age:** Boost Quantum Action recharge rate (+50/+100/+200 per hour per building)
- **Middle Ages:** Boost Quantum Coin production instead
  - Tier 1 (Early MA): No extra bonus
  - Tier 2 (High MA): +10% Coin production bonus
  - Tier 3 (Late MA): +20% Coin production bonus

All Middle Ages residential buildings are 2×2.

**Middle Ages examples:**
- Early MA: Multistorey House, Frame House, Clapboard House
- High MA: Mansion, Brownstone House, Town House (+10%)
- Late MA: Estate House, Apartment House, Manor (+20%)

### Production Buildings
Produce Quantum Coins and Quantum Supplies. In Middle Ages also grant supply production boosts:
- High MA tier: +10% Supply production
- Late MA tier: +20% Supply production
- Tiers 2 and 3 require **Chrono Alloys**

**Middle Ages examples:**
- Early MA: Tannery, Farm, Brewery
- High MA: Shoemaker, Alchemist, Spice Trader (+10%)
- Late MA: Bakery, Windmill, Cooperage (+20%)

### Cultural Buildings
- Provide **Euphoria** (production multiplier)
- **Iron Age:** Only provide Euphoria
- **Middle Ages:** Also generate bonus **Quantum Actions per recharge cycle** (role swap from residential buildings in Iron Age)

**Middle Ages examples:** Gallows, Pillory, Library, Printer, Doctor, Cartographer, Palace  
Note: Championship 1 2026 rebalanced all cultural buildings to diversify usage.

### Decorations
- **Unique mechanic:** Reduce Euphoria (drain it) unlike the main city
- In exchange: provide **combat boosts** (Attack and/or Defense bonuses for QI armies)
- Boost range: 10%–45% depending on decoration type

**Decoration specializations:**
- Attack-focused (red boost): e.g., Signpost (1×1)
- Defense-focused (blue boost): e.g., Gargoyle (1×1)
- Balanced: e.g., Nautical Statue, Group of Trees (2×2), Tower Ruins (2×2)

### Goods Buildings (selected 3 of 5 in Middle Ages)
Produce one specific Quantum Good type used for donation/negotiation nodes.  
Variable production durations: 1 second to 10 hours at different output levels.

**Middle Ages goods types:**
- Quantum Copper — Copper Foundry
- Quantum Honey — Beekeeper
- Quantum Bricks — Brickworks
- Quantum Rope — Ropery
- Quantum Gunpowder — Gunpowder Manufactory

**Iron Age goods types:**
- Ebony (Ebony Woodworks), Iron (Iron Foundry), Jewelry (Jewelry Manufacturer), Limestone (Limestone Mason), Cloth (Weaving Mill)

### Military Buildings (selected 3 of 5 unit categories in Middle Ages)
Produce specific unit types for combat encounters.

**Middle Ages unit categories and examples:**
- **Light:** Mercenaries
- **Ranged:** Mounted Archers
- **Fast:** Heavy Cavalry
- **Heavy:** Armored Infantry
- **Artillery:** Catapults (Early MA) → Trebuchets → Cannons (higher tiers)

## 5. Euphoria System

Euphoria is the QI equivalent of Happiness in the main city. Generated by Cultural buildings, drained by Decorations.

| State | Ratio Range | Production Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| Rebelling | Below 20% | 0.2× (20%) |
| Unruly | 21–60% | 0.6× (60%) |
| Unhappy | 61–80% | 0.8× (80%) |
| Neutral | 81–120% | 1.0× (100%) |
| Content | 121–140% | 1.1× (110%) |
| Happy | 141–200% | 1.2× (120%) |
| Enthusiastic | Over 200% | 1.5× (150%) |

**Applies to:** Quantum Coins, Quantum Supplies AND Chrono Alloy production from Residential and Production buildings (official announcement: "sets a mood which determines a multiplier for productions of Quantum Coins, Quantum Supplies and Chrono Alloys").  
**Does NOT apply to:** Town Hall production.

**Core tradeoff:** Adding Decorations reduces Euphoria (and thus production output) but increases combat performance on the map.

### Verified production formula (2026-07, matched against FoE Helper citymap.js and live-city numbers)

```
production = round( base × (euphoriaMult + totalBoost/100) )   — rounded per building
```

- Euphoria multiplier and the summed % boosts are **ADDITIVE**, not multiplicative.
- `totalBoost` = sum of in-settlement building boosts (`guild_raids_coins_production` /
  `guild_raids_supplies_production`) **plus** main-city buildings with QI bonuses
  (Ascended event buildings — FoE Helper's `Boosts.Sums` includes both grids).
- Chrono Alloy has no % boost type → gets `base × euphoriaMult` only.
- Town Hall: flat 50,000/50,000/15, no euphoria, no boost.
- FoE Helper's city-map top bar only *displays* the in-settlement boost sums, but its math
  uses the combined total — which is why displayed 370%/20% yields per-building
  multipliers of (1.5 + 4.06) and (1.5 + 0.44) when the main city adds 36%/24%.
- Verified to the digit against a real city: 35× Alkimista + Sörfőzde + 20× Favázas épület +
  3× Deszkaburkolatú ház + Kúria + 6× Kuruzsló + 3× Pellengér, euphoria ×1.5 →
  coins 1,783,875 / supplies 2,878,928 / alloy 7,385 / AP 69,200 per cycle.

## 6. Resources

### Quantum Actions (QA)
The core "action point" resource for the map.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Cap | 100,000 |
| Base recharge rate | 5,000 per hour |
| Iron Age residential boost | +50/+100/+200 per hour per building |
| Middle Ages cultural boost | +QA per recharge cycle (varies by building) |

- Costs vary based on distance between nodes and difficulty level
- Can be **purchased with Quantum Shards** (price increases per purchase; resets each season)
- Movement cost was reduced from 500 to 100 per node transition in Championship 1, 2025

### Quantum Shards
Premium currency within QI.

**Sources:** Clearing node encounters, Quantum Pass milestones, purchasing with Diamonds  
**Uses:** Speed up settlement production; purchase additional Quantum Actions; general acceleration

### Quantum Coins
Primary building currency. Produced by Town Hall, Residential, and Production buildings. Affected by Euphoria.

### Quantum Supplies
Secondary building currency. Produced by Town Hall and Production buildings. Affected by Euphoria.

### Quantum Goods (5 types per era)
Used for donation/negotiation nodes. Produced by selected Goods buildings.

### Chrono Alloys (Middle Ages only, introduced October 2025)
- Required for Tier 2 and Tier 3 buildings
- Produced by: Town Hall, Residential buildings, Production buildings
- Acts as a gating resource for higher-tier construction

### Lunar Coins (Middle Ages only, introduced October 2025)
- Earned from every node interaction on the map
- Maximum storage: 200,000
- **Carry over between seasons** (do not reset)
- Spent in the **Quantum Store** to purchase specific building fragments of your choice
- Replaced the previous random fragment drop system

### Quantum Medals
Also spendable in the Quantum Store (Middle Ages era).

## 7. The QI Map

### Structure
A branching network of nodes. Each difficulty level has its own segment leading to a Boss node. Defeating the Boss unlocks the next difficulty.

### Node Types

**Battle Nodes (Combat)**
- **Standard Strength:** Average-strength enemy army
- **Enhanced Strength:** Stronger enemies; if uncleared they buff the Boss; if cleared they strengthen the guild against the Boss
- Color-coded **red** (offensive) or **blue** (defensive):
  - Red nodes: Require an attacking army; benefit from Attack (red) boosts
  - Blue nodes: Require a defending army; benefit from Defense (blue) boosts
- **Special unit-type challenges:** Bonus rewards (double progress + loot) for winning using only a specified unit type

**Donation / Negotiation Nodes**
- Require contributions of Quantum Coins, Quantum Supplies, Quantum Goods, or units
- Also cost Quantum Actions to interact with
- Higher difficulty levels demand more and different goods combinations

**Garrison Nodes**
- Maximum **2 per difficulty level**
- Upon completion: grant a **50% Attack and Defense Boost** to the completing player
- High-value strategic targets

**Stronghold Nodes**
- Maximum **2 per difficulty level**
- Provide a significant boost to the entire guild upon completion

**Boss Node**
- Located at the end of each difficulty segment
- Significantly stronger than normal nodes
- Shared HP pool across the entire guild — each member's attack permanently damages it
- Must be cleared to unlock the next difficulty level

**Farming Nodes** (introduced 4th Championship)
- Become available after a Boss is defeated
- 3 nodes with 1,000,000 progress capacity each
- Allow continued resource/progress farming after a difficulty is completed

### Map Navigation
- Moving between adjacent nodes costs Quantum Actions
- Longer distances and higher difficulties cost more
- A **Quantum Officer** can mark nodes to guide guild members

### Node Progress
- Each guild member contributing to a node adds to a **shared progress bar**
- Progress requirements scale with guild size (170–700 progress points for normal nodes depending on member count, as of Championship 1, 2025)
- Every interaction with any node rewards **10 Progress Points** to the individual player

## 8. Combat Boost Types

### In-QI Settlement Decoration Boosts
Placed in the settlement at the cost of Euphoria:
- Red/attack boosts (e.g., Signpost)
- Blue/defense boosts (e.g., Gargoyle)
- Balanced (e.g., Nautical Statue, Group of Trees)
- Range: 10%–45% per decoration

### Garrison Node Completion Boost
- **+50% Attack AND Defense** for the completing player (temporary, per-encounter)

### Main City Buildings That Apply in QI
Most main-city combat boosts do NOT apply in QI. Known exceptions:

| Building | QI Boost |
|---|---|
| Forgotten Temple (28-day event building) | +50% Atk & Def for BOTH red and blue troops |
| Tourney Grounds (14-day event building) | +25% Atk & Def for BOTH red and blue troops |
| Yggdrasil (Level 9) | +50% Attack for red troops only |
| Shinto Temple (Level 9) | +40% Attack, +20% Defense for blue troops only |
| Royal Bathhouse (Level 6) | +20% Atk & Def red; +60% Atk, +20% Def blue |
| Sun Temple (Level 9) | +50% Defense for red troops only |
| Mughal Temple (Level 6) | +60% Defense for blue troops only |
| Hut of the Sacred Instruments (Level 9) | +30% Attack for red troops only |

Multiple qualifying buildings of the same type stack.

### Neo Buildings (earned through QI, placed in main city)
Provide QI-specific bonuses:

| Building | Notable Bonus |
|---|---|
| Neo Colossus (up to L11) | Atk/Def for attacking & defending in QI; also boosts GBG & GE |
| Neo Checkmate Square (2 levels) | 34–63% Atk & Def for attacking & defending |
| Neo Aviary (2 levels) | 25–41% Atk & Def with motivated unit production bonuses |
| Neo Winners' Plaza (2 levels) | Defending & attacking army boosts; GBG bonus |
| Neo Marble Gateway (2 levels) | Army performance boosts (fragments from Neo Colossus L8/9/10) |
| Neo Botanical Rotunda (2 levels) | Forge Points + Puzzle Energy + army bonuses |

Percentages are age-scaled (range 8%–53% depending on player era and building level).

## 9. Quantum Officer Role

A designated guild role assigned by Guild Leader/Founders. Responsibilities:
- Selecting which incursion map/path the guild enters
- Unlocking the next difficulty after the Boss is defeated
- Marking and unmarking nodes on the map to guide guildmates
- Setting strategic direction for resource allocation

## 10. Progress Points and Individual Rewards

### Earning Progress Points
- Every interaction with any node (combat or donation): **+10 Progress Points**
- Special unit-type challenge completion: **double progress** for that encounter

### Free Reward Chests (per Season)
- Reach **40 Progress Points** → 1 free Reward Chest
- Reach **160 Progress Points** → 1 additional free Reward Chest
- Chest content improves based on the highest difficulty the guild has cleared that season

### End-of-Incursion Guild Chests
- 10 tiered chests (Chest 1 through Chest 10)
- Unlocked based on combined guild Progress Points during the incursion
- Example: Chest 10 requires ~150,000 combined guild progress points
- Content scales with tier

## 11. The Quantum Pass

A championship-spanning (not seasonal) progression track:
- Progress carries over from season to season throughout an entire 6-season Championship
- Resets only at the start of a new Championship
- New rewards every **30 Progress Points**

| Tier | Cost | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Free Pass | Free | Base rewards at each milestone |
| Silver Pass | Purchasable | Additional exclusive rewards |
| Gold Pass | Purchasable | Additional rewards beyond Silver |

### Leaderboard Mechanics
- Guilds ranked by collective progress points

## 13. Middle Ages Era (October 2, 2025 Update)

A major expansion adding the Middle Ages as the second QI era, combining three sub-eras into one.

### Key Changes from Iron Age

| Aspect | Iron Age | Middle Ages |
|---|---|---|
| QA boost source | Residential buildings | Cultural buildings |
| Coin boost source | Production buildings | Residential buildings |
| New resource | — | Chrono Alloys (for Tier 2/3 buildings) |
| Reward currency | Random fragment drops | Lunar Coins → Quantum Store (player choice) |
| Military selection | Specific building types | Unit categories (all buildings of type unlocked) |
| Unit donation nodes | Present | Removed |
| Building selection | 2 goods + 2 military | 3 goods + 3 military unit categories |

### New Resources
- **Chrono Alloys** — gates Tier 2/3 construction; produced by Town Hall, Residential, Production buildings
- **Lunar Coins** — earned from every node action; carry over between seasons; spent in Quantum Store

### Quantum Store
Allows players to purchase specific building fragments instead of relying on random drops — a major quality-of-life improvement for targeted building upgrades.
